Yafen 雅芬 · 红杉树舞蹈团艺术顾问


雅芬(Yafen)民族女高音歌唱家，国家一级演员，中外文化交流使者。 先后就读于苏州评弹学校、中国音乐学院、解放军艺术学院，师从声乐名家金铁霖老师。 多次登台央视春晚、元宵节、国庆、中秋等国家级大型晚会。 2005年、2024年、2025年相继在国内及新西兰举办个人独唱音乐会， 先后出版《老乡见老乡》《江南情韵》《芬芳中华》多张个人演唱专辑。

2016年随先生到澳大利亚，深耕中澳文化交流，积极推广中国民歌。 曾受邀参演2017年中澳旅游年开幕式， 携手中国歌剧舞剧院民乐团及昆士兰、阿德莱德、悉尼等城市交响乐团， 在澳洲各大艺术殿堂演绎经典民乐民歌， 被澳大利亚文化媒体盛赞为“来自东方丝绸般的美妙歌声”。


Yafen is a Chinese soprano, cultural exchange ambassador, member of the China Musicians Association, and Charity Ambassador for the China Disabled Persons’ Federation.

She graduated from Suzhou Pingtan Art School and the PLA Academy of Arts, and later pursued advanced studies at the China Conservatory of Music under the guidance of distinguished vocal educators, including the renowned Professor Jin Tielin, former President of the China Conservatory of Music.

Throughout her career, Yafen has been a featured performer on many of China’s most prestigious national stages, including the Spring Festival Gala, Lantern Festival Gala, and National Day Gala presented by the Ministry of Culture and China Central Television (CCTV). She has collaborated with China Record Corporation and Guangzhou New Times Audio & Video Publishing House to release several albums, including When Fellow Townsmen Meet, Charm of Southern China, and Fragrance of China.

In 2005, she presented her first solo concert, Charm of Southern China · Patriotism. In 2024, she staged her second major solo production, the symphonic vocal recital Daughter of Southern China. Her performances of Pingtan-inspired works such as Butterflies Fly in Pairs Again and Millennium Promise, together with the song Colourful Wings to the Ninth Heaven, have gained wide popularity and have played a significant role in promoting the culture and tourism of her hometown.

Since 2016, Yafen has been actively performing throughout Australia. She has collaborated with the China National Opera & Dance Drama Theatre during its Australian tour, as well as with the Queensland Symphony Orchestra, Adelaide Symphony Orchestra, and Sydney Symphony Orchestra. Her performances have included Chinese New Year concerts and the Opening Ceremony of the 2017 China–Australia Year of Tourism, appearing at major venues such as Llewellyn Hall (Australian National University, Canberra), the Queensland Performing Arts Centre, Adelaide Festival Centre, and the Sydney Opera House.

In 2025, she presented the art song recital Daughter of the Orient in New Zealand. Her singing has been praised by the media as possessing “a beautiful voice, like silk drifting from the Orient,” reflecting both the elegance of her artistry and the distinctive charm of Chinese vocal music.
